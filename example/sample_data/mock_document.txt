========================================
Rotational Inertia Measurement Experiment
========================================

[Experiment Purpose]
To measure the moment of inertia of a rigid body using a rotational inertia apparatus and verify the rotational law.

[Theory]
According to the rotational law, the angular acceleration α of a rigid body rotating around a fixed axis is proportional to the net torque M and inversely proportional to the moment of inertia I:
M = I·α

In this experiment, weights are suspended from the tower wheel via a string, producing a torque M = mgr.

[Equipment]
- Rotational inertia instrument
- Digital millisecond timer
- Weights (20g, 40g, 60g)
- Vernier caliper
- Stopwatch

[Procedure]
1. Level the rotational inertia apparatus.
2. Select a weight and record its mass m.
3. Measure the radius r of the tower wheel.
4. Release the weight and measure the angular acceleration α.
5. Repeat measurements with different masses.
6. Calculate the moment of inertia I and compare with theoretical values.

[Data Processing]
I = mgr/α
Relative error = |I_measured - I_theoretical| / I_theoretical × 100%
